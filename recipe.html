Yam and Egg sauce

Yam and egg sauce is a typical nigerian breakfast meal rich in carbohydrates, protein and many other nutrients. Yam and egg sauce are a delicious food combination. It is a popular food combo that is eaten in Nigeria and many parts of the world. First of all, yams are rich in carbohydrates and fiber but low in protein and do not contain fat. It is also rich in vitamin C, folate, thiamine, and vitamin B5. Yams are typically high in copper, potassium, manganese, and magnesium. Eggs are rich in vitamin A, vitamin B5, vitamin B12, vitamin B2, and folate. They also contain some minerals such as selenium, phosphorus, calcium, and zinc.

Ingredients Measurement
Tuber of yam 1
Eggs 4-6
Medium-sized tomatoes 3-4
Scotch bonnet(rodo) 2-3
Onion 1
Vegetable oil ⅓ cup
Salt ½ tsp plus 1 tsp for yam
Seasoning cube 1
Curry and Thyme As you wish (It's optional)
    How to prepare: Yam
  • Slice yam into smaller pieces of your choice and peel the skin off.
  • Rinse in clean water till water runs clear.
  • Put yam in a pan, add enough water to cover it.
  • Add salt and place on medium heat and cook till soft.
  • Test with a fork, if it goes through, then the yam should be soft enough.
  • Drain water from yam if you want.
    • Egg Sauce:
  • Break eggs into a shallow bowl.
  • Whisk till egg white and egg yolk are well combined, add salt and whisk to combine then set it aside.
  • Chop or blend the tomatoes, scotch bonnet, and onions together and then set aside.
  • Place a frying pan on a medium/low heat, add vegetable oil, heat for about 1-2 minutes.
  • Add chopped or blended tomatoes, peppers and onions (this should sizzle).
  • Cook for about 4-5 minutes or till oil floats on the sauce.
  • Add salt, seasoning, curry and thyme. Stir to combine ingredient with sauce and also avoid sauce sticking to the pan.
  • Add whisked eggs to sauce and leave for about a minute, this will allow the egg to harden.
  • Stir to combine sauce with the eggs and leave for few more seconds.
